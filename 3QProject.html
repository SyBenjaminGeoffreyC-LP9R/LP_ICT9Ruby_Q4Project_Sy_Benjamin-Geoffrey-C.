Rick Riordan

Richard Russell Riordan Jr.—also known as Rick Riordan—is an American author, best known for writing the Percy Jackson & the Olympians series.

Rick Riordan was originally a teacher before starting his writing career. After teaching middle school in Texas and California for about 15 years, Riordan quit to dedicate himself to writing full-time.

Rick Riordan, dubbed "storyteller of the gods" by Publishers Weekly, is the author of five #1 New York Times best selling middle grade series with millions of copies sold throuhgout the world.

His most popular book series include: Percy Jackson and the Olympians, The Heroes of Olympus, The Trials of Apollo, based on Greek and Roman Mythology; The Kane Chronicles, based on Egyptian Mythology; and Magnus Chase and the Gods of Asgard, based on Norse Mythology.

His first popular book for middle graders—Percy Jackson and the Olympians—originally started out as a bedtime story for his son, Haley Riordan, who has ADHD (attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder) and dyslexia (a reading disability) to encourage him to read, with the main character (Percy) being based of him.
